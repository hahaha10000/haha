Guangzhou Transportation Plan

Transportation Notes

  • Walking and cycling are the primary modes of transportation. Firstly, they reduce communication costs for foreign visitors, and secondly, they allow you to better appreciate the charm of Guangzhou. For longer distances between tourist destinations, road transportation such as taxis is recommended.
  • The subway lines 1 and 6 are the main ones. The nearest subway exits will be marked at relevant locations.

Transportation Plan

Day 1

(1) Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall → March 29 Uprising Headquarters Site (6-minute bike ride)
(2) March 29 Uprising Headquarters Site → Huanghuagang Martyrs’ Cemetery (8-minute drive)
(3) Huanghuagang Martyrs’ Cemetery → Sanyuanli Anti-British Museum (12-minute drive)

Day 2

(1) All-China Federation of Trade Unions Building → Provincial-Hong Kong Strike Memorial Hall (3-minute walk)
(2) Provincial-Hong Kong Strike Memorial Hall → CPC Guangdong Regional Committee Memorial (4-minute bike ride)
(3) CPC Guangdong Regional Committee Memorial → Yang Pao’an Former Residence (8-minute bike ride)
(4) Yang Pao’an Former Residence → Peasant Movement Training Institute (7-minute bike ride)
(5) Peasant Movement Training Institute → CPC Third National Congress Site (14-minute bike ride)

Day 3

(1) Guangzhou Uprising Memorial Hall → Guangzhou Uprising Martyrs’ Cemetery (13-minute bike ride)
(2) Guangzhou Uprising Martyrs’ Cemetery → Whampoa Military Academy (38-minute drive)

Day 4

(1) Sacred Heart Cathedral → Shamian Island (12-minute bike ride)
[Shamian Island includes:
  • Former Tait & Co. Site
  • Our Lady of Lourdes Chapel
  • Former HSBC Building
  • Former German Consulate

  • Former French Police Station
  • Losee & Co., Anderson, Meyer & Co., and Butterfield & Swire
  • Former Bank of Taiwan and Sassoon House
(2) Shamian Island exploration: Walk between sites as listed.
