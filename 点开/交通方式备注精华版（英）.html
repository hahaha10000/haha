Guangzhou Travel Transportation Plan

Transportation Notes

  1. Walking and cycling are the main transportation methods. Firstly, this reduces communication costs for foreign travelers; secondly, it allows for a better appreciation of Guangzhou's urban charm. When tourist destinations are far apart, road transportation such as taxis is recommended.
  2. Metro Line 1 and Line 6 are the main lines used. The nearest metro exits are provided for relevant locations.

Travel Transportation Plan

Route 1

1. Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall —— Huanghuagang 72 Martyrs Cemetery （9 minutes by car）

Route 2

1. Site of the Peasant Movement Institute (founded by Comrade Mao Zedong) —— Former Site of All-China Federation of Trade Unions （6 minutes by bike）
2. Former Site of All-China Federation of Trade Unions —— Memorial Hall of the 3rd National Congress of the Communist Party of China （10 minutes by taxi）

Route 3

1. Guangzhou Uprising Martyrs Cemetery —— Former Site of Whampoa Military Academy Memorial Hall （38 minutes by car）

Route 4

1. Sacred Heart Cathedral —— Our Lady of Lourdes Chapel (Guangzhou Diocese, Shamian Island) （10 minutes by bike）
2. All destinations within Shamian Island are roughly close to each other, so just follow the route order on foot.
