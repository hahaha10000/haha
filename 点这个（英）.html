Guangzhou Red History and Architectural Culture Tour

This platform integrates the historical context of modern revolutionary history and colonial-era architectural heritage in Guangzhou, guiding you through key nodes and cultural landmarks in the city's memory.

Featured Tours

Four themed routes connect key sites from modern resistance struggles to colonial history. Click the button below to view detailed itineraries.

Historical Architecture Tour

· Sacred Heart Cathedral: A fully stone Gothic structure completed in 1863, blending Chinese and Western craftsmanship.
· Shamian Island Complex: Over 150 European-style buildings from the late 19th century, including Gothic and Neoclassical styles.
· Whampoa Military Academy: The cradle of the 1924 Kuomintang-Communist cooperation, witnessing the Northern Expedition and War of Resistance.

Red Tourism Routes

· Modern Resistance Route: Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall (Two Protection Movements) → Huanghuagang 72 Martyrs' Cemetery (1911 Uprising)
· Workers' Movement Route: Former Site of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions (Hong Kong-Canton Strike) → Peasant Movement Institute (Mao Zedong trained peasant movement leaders)
· Revolutionary Uprising Route: Guangzhou Uprising Memorial Hall (1927 Soviet Regime) → Whampoa Military Academy
· Colonial History Route: Sacred Heart Cathedral → Shamian Island (British and French Concession Buildings)

Historical Events

· Sanyuanli Anti-British Movement (1841): China's first large-scale victory against foreign aggression in modern history
· Hong Kong-Canton Strike (1925-1926): A 16-month anti-imperialist workers' movement
· Guangzhou Uprising (1927): An attempt to establish China's first urban Soviet regime
· Shaji Massacre (1925): A colonial violence incident during an anti-imperialist demonstration
