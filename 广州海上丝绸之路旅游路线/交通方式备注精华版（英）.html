Guangzhou Transportation Plan

Transportation Plan

First Route: Qin and Han Origins and Ancient Ports (200 BC - 6th century AD)

(1) Huangpu Ancient Port Site → Guangxiao Temple (30-minute drive)
(2) Guangxiao Temple → Western Han Nanyue King Palace Museum (Take Metro Line 1 from Ximenkou Station to Gongyuanqian Station, Exit E, then 5-minute walk, total about 15 minutes)

Second Route: Tang and Song Prosperity and Municipal Shipping Office Culture (7th century - 13th century)

(1) Huaisheng Mosque → Guangzhou Museum (12-minute drive)
(2) Guangzhou Museum → Xianxian Mosque (8-minute bike ride)

Third Route: Ming and Qing Trade and Thirteen Hongs Era (14th century - 19th century)

(1) Jinlun Guild Hall → Shamian Island (7-minute drive)
(2) Shamian Island → Guangzhou Thirteen Hongs Museum (4-minute bike ride)

Fourth Route: Modern Shipping and Maritime Silk Road Culture Inheritance (20th century - present)

(1) Guangzhou Maritime Museum → Nansha International Cruise Home Port (49-minute drive)

Note: Historical details are cross-referenced with official records and architectural studies to ensure accuracy.
