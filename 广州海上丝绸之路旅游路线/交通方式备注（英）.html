Travel Itinerary & Transportation Details

DAY 1: Theme---Qin-Han Origins & Ancient Ports (200 BC – 6th century AD)

1. Nanhai Temple → Huangpu Ancient Port Heritage Site （40-minute drive）
2. Huangpu Ancient Port Heritage Site → Guangta Temple （51-minute drive）
3. Guangta Temple → Guangxiao Temple （2-minute walk from Exit C of Ximenkou Station, Metro Line 1; estimated from transport info）
4. Guangxiao Temple → Museum of the Nanyue King’s Palace （Take Metro Line 1 from Ximenkou Station to Gongyuanqian Station, 5-minute walk from Exit E; ~15 minutes total）

DAY 2: Theme---Tang-Song Prosperity & Maritime Trade Office Culture (7th – 13th century)

1. Huaisheng Mosque → Liurong Temple （4-minute cycle）
2. Liurong Temple → Guangzhou Museum （8-minute cycle）
3. Guangzhou Museum → Xianxian Mosque （8-minute cycle）

DAY 3: Theme---Ming-Qing Trade & Thirteen Hongs Era (14th – 19th century)

1. Jinlun Guild Hall → Hualin Temple （2-minute walk）
2. Hualin Temple → Shamian Island （7-minute cycle）
3. Shamian Island → Guangzhou Thirteen Hongs Museum （4-minute cycle）

DAY 4: Theme---Modern Shipping & Maritime Silk Road Heritage (20th century – present)

1. Guangdong Museum → Guangzhou Maritime Museum （37-minute drive）
2. Guangzhou Maritime Museum → Nansha International Cruise Homeport （49-minute drive）
