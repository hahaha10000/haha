Guangzhou Maritime Silk Road Tour

Guangzhou, a pivotal node on the ancient Maritime Silk Road, boasts a rich heritage of maritime trade spanning over two millennia. From Qin-Han origins to modern shipping, this route traces the city's evolution as a global trading hub, showcasing temples, ports, relics, and museums that tell the story of cultural exchange and commercial prosperity.

Featured Attractions

A 4-day journey covering Qin-Han origins, Tang-Song prosperity, Ming-Qing trade, and modern maritime heritage. Click below to explore detailed itineraries.

Maritime Heritage Sites

· Temple of the South Sea God: China's only intact ancient official sea temple, known as the "Forest of Steles of the South" with 1,400+ years of history
· Huangpu Ancient Port: Qing Dynasty's sole foreign trade port under the "Single Port Policy,"见证ing ships like the "Götheborg"
· Huaisheng Mosque: Tang-era mosque with the iconic Light Tower, a navigational beacon for Arab traders

Maritime Silk Road Itinerary

· Day 1: Qin-Han Origins & Ancient Ports: Temple of the South Sea God → Huangpu Ancient Port → Guangxiao Temple → Nanyue Kingdom Palace Museum
· Day 2: Tang-Song Prosperity: Huaisheng Mosque → Liurong Temple Pagoda → Guangzhou Museum → Islamic Sage Tomb
· Day 3: Ming-Qing Trade: Jinlun Guild Hall → Hualin Temple → Shamian Island → Thirteen-Hongs Museum
· Day 4: Modern Maritime Heritage: Guangdong Museum → Guangzhou Maritime Museum → Nansha Cruise Homeport

Key Historical Highlights

· 594 CE: Temple of the South Sea God established, becoming a ritual hub for maritime trade
· 1757: Huangpu Port becomes China's sole foreign trade port under the "Single Port Policy"
· 19th Century: Shamian Island emerges as a colonial trade enclave, reflecting Western influence
· 2019: Nansha Cruise Homeport opens, linking modern Guangzhou to global maritime networks
